What is phonics?

Developed in consultation with phonics teachers and academics, Learn to read with Pip is a complete, phase-by-phase systematic synthetic phonics programme that will build on your child's phonics experiences in the classroom.

It is delivered through a series of fun, daily 10-minute learning sessions that the young learner can complete on either an iPad or a computer.

What is blending?

Learn to read with Pip is appropriate for children between 3-8 years old.

Your child will:

  • Learn the letters of the alphabet and respective sounds
  • Learn sounds for combinations of letters
  • Blend together sounds to read words
  • Segment sounds in words to spell them
  • Practise common ‘high frequency’ words
  • Move from simple CVC words to more complex words
  • Learn alternative pronunciations for combinations of letters
  • Learn alternative spelling techniques for sounds

Keywords? What on earth are they?

Each unit of five sessions culminates in its own unique story, which allows the young learner to apply their new skills to a real-life context: reading, understanding and enjoying a story.

All the stories have been creatively led by children’s storywriter Luke Kelly, grandson of Roald Dahl.

CVC, CVCC, CCVC...

Learning is only useful when it is applied to real life situations which are meaningful for the learner.

Throughout the programme your child combines their newly acquired skills with their creative imagination to create their very own Magic Book, filled with letters, words and pictures.

For Parents

We want to de-mystify for parents the process young learners undertake when they learn to read, including phonics, so they feel more comfortable being involved.

We are creating a bank of online information which explain all the main concepts and provide access to the child’s progress through the programme via our website.
