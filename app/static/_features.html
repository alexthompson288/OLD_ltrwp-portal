Overview

Developed in consultation with leading phonics teachers, academics and experts from around the UK, Learn to read with Pip is a complete, phase-by-phase systematic synthetic phonics programme that will build on your child's phonics experiences in the classroom.

We have tried to simplify everything we cover by structuring the information into sessions and units. You can think of a session as a day and a unit as a week as there are five sessions in every unit. A single session is a fun, daily 10-minute learning period with a particular learning objective that a child can complete on either an iPad or a computer.

A parent or teacher can download the digital programme through our website or through the Apple App Store and must create an account for their child. Once this step has been completed a child is able to explore and learn either by themselves or alongside an adult.

Curriculum

Learn to read with Pip is appropriate for children between 3 and 8 years old.

Your child will:

  • Learn the letters of the alphabet and respective sounds
  • Learn sounds for combinations of letters
  • Blend together sounds to read words
  • Segment sounds in words to spell them
  • Practise common ‘high frequency’ words
  • Move from simple CVC words to more complex words
  • Learn alternative pronunciations for combinations of letters
  • Learn alternative spelling techniques for sounds

Approach

Learn to read with Pip consists of 240 daily learning sessions, each of which the young learner can complete at their own pace.

Each daily session is designed to last for about 10 minutes.

Each session contains one distinct learning objective and follows the teaching sequence: Revise, Learn, Practise, Apply.

Stories

Each unit (basically a week) of five sessions (one per day) culminates in its own unique story, which allows the young learner to apply their new skills to a real-life context: reading, understanding and enjoying a story.

The complexity of language in the stories grows with the developing abilities of the child.

All the stories have been creatively designed by children’s storywriter Luke Kelly, grandson of Roald Dahl.

Magic Book

Learning is most enjoyable when it is applied to real life situations which are meaningful for the learner.

Throughout the programme your child combines their newly acquired skills with their creative imagination to create their very own Magic Book, filled with letters, words and pictures.
